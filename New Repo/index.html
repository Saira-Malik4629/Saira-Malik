5 Main Types of Animals

  1. Mammals:

    Mammals are warm-blooded vertebrates characterized by having hair or fur,
    breathing air, possessing mammary glands to produce milk, and having a four-chambered heart.

  2. Fishes:

    Vertebrates are animals with a vertebral column and a cranium, or skull.

  3. Birds:

    Birds are a group of warm-blooded vertebrates constituting the class Aves, characterised
    by feathers, toothless beaked jaws, the laying of hard-shelled eggs.

  4. Reptiles:

    Reptiles, as commonly defined, are a group of tetrapods with an ectothermic metabolism
    and amniotic development. Living traditional reptiles comprise four orders: Testudines, Crocodilia,
    Squamata, and Rhynchocephalia.
